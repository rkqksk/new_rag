**BANT Qualification Framework**:

**B**udget: Can they afford it?
```
Questions:
- What's your budget for this initiative?
- Who controls the budget?
- When is budget allocated?

Red flags:
- No budget allocated
- Budget < minimum deal size
```

**A**uthority: Can they make decisions?
```
Questions:
- Who else is involved in the decision?
- What's the approval process?
- Have you purchased similar solutions before?

Decision-makers (manufacturing):
- Procurement Manager
- Operations Director
- Plant Manager
- CFO (large deals)
```

**N**eed: Do they have a problem?
```
Questions:
- What's the biggest challenge you're facing?
- What happens if you don't solve this?
- What have you tried already?

Strong needs:
- Regulatory compliance
- Cost reduction > 20%
- Capacity bottleneck
```

**T**imeline: When will they buy?
```
Questions:
- When do you need this implemented?
- What's driving the timeline?
- Are there any deadlines?

Timelines:
- Immediate (< 1 month): Hot
- Short-term (1-3 months): Warm
- Long-term (> 6 months): Nurture
```

**Lead Scoring** (0-100 scale):
```
Fit Score (50 points):
- Industry match: 20 pts

Engagement Score (50 points):

Total Score:
80-100: Hot (contact immediately)
60-79: Warm (nurture with content)

```

### 3. Discovery & Needs Analysis

**Discovery Call Framework** (30-45 min):

**1. Rapport Building** (5 min):
- Company background
- Role & responsibilities
- Recent industry trends

**2. Current State** (10 min):
- Current process/solution
- Pain points & challenges
- Quantify impact (cost, time, quality)

**3. Desired State** (10 min):
- Ideal solution
- Success metrics
- Timeline & budget

**4. Decision Process** (10 min):
- Stakeholders involved
- Evaluation criteria
- Next steps

**5. Next Steps** (5 min):
- Schedule demo/presentation
- Share resources
- Set follow-up

**Power Questions**:
```
Problem Identification:
- "What's the biggest challenge in your production process?"
- "How much is this costing you per month?"
- "What have you tried to solve this?"

Impact Questions:
- "What happens if you don't solve this in the next 6 months?"
- "How would solving this impact your team?"
- "What metrics would improve?"

Vision Questions:
- "What would the ideal solution look like?"
- "How would you measure success?"
- "What would make this a home run?"
```

### 4. Demo & Presentation Best Practices

**Demo Structure** (45-60 min):

**1. Introduction** (5 min):
- Agenda overview
- Confirm attendees & roles
- Recap discovery insights

**2. Business Case** (10 min):
- Problem statement (their words)
- Impact quantification
- Solution overview

**3. Product Demo** (25 min):
- Feature → Benefit → Their use case
- Focus on top 3 pain points
- Interactive (let them drive)

**4. ROI & Value** (10 min):
- Cost savings calculation
- Time savings
- Quality improvements
- Payback period

**5. Next Steps** (10 min):
- Q&A
- Trial/POC discussion
- Proposal timeline

**Demo Do's & Don'ts**:

✅ **Do**:
- Customize to their industry
- Show real data (anonymized customer)
- Address their specific pain points
- Keep it simple (80% talk, 20% click)

❌ **Don't**:
- Generic feature tour
- Overpromise capabilities
- Technical deep-dives (unless requested)
- Skip ROI discussion

### 5. Proposal & Pricing

**Proposal Structure**:
```
1. Executive Summary
   - Problem statement
   - Recommended solution
   - Expected ROI

2. Needs Assessment
   - Discovery findings
   - Pain points identified
   - Success criteria

3. Proposed Solution
   - Product/service description
   - Implementation plan
   - Timeline

4. Investment & ROI
   - Pricing (itemized)
   - Payment terms
   - ROI calculation

5. Case Studies
   - Similar customer success stories
   - Measurable results

6. Next Steps
   - Acceptance timeline
   - Implementation kickoff
   - Support & training

7. Terms & Conditions
```

**Pricing Strategies**:

**Tiered Pricing** (Good-Better-Best):
```
Starter: $999/month
- 1,000 API calls
- 1 GB storage
- 1 user
→ Anchor (makes Pro look valuable)

Professional: $2,499/month ⭐ Most Popular
- 100,000 API calls
- 50 GB storage
- 10 users
→ Target tier (most conversions)

Enterprise: $9,999/month
- Unlimited calls
- 500 GB storage
- Unlimited users
→ Upsell target
```

**Value-Based Pricing**:
```
Customer's annual pain cost: $500,000
Your solution saves: 40% ($200,000/year)
Your annual price: $50,000 (25% of savings)
Customer ROI: 300% (3× return)
```

**Negotiation Levers**:
- Volume discounts (> 20% price reduction)
- Multi-year contracts (20-30% discount)
- Payment terms (Net 30 vs. Net 60)
- Implementation support (included vs. paid)
- Feature tiers (hold back premium features)

### 6. Objection Handling

**Common Objections & Responses**:

**"It's too expensive"**:
```
Response: "I understand budget is a concern. Let's look at the ROI..."
- Show cost of inaction
- Break down monthly cost (vs. annual)
- Compare to current solution cost
- Offer tiered pricing
```

**"We need to think about it"**:
```
Response: "Of course, this is an important decision. What specifically do you need to think about?"
- Identify the real objection
- Provide additional information
- Set specific follow-up date
```

**"We're happy with our current solution"**:
```
Response: "That's great to hear! Out of curiosity, what do you like most about it?"
- Find gaps in current solution
- Position as upgrade, not replacement
- Share competitive advantages
```

**"Not the right time"**:
```
Response: "I appreciate that. When would be a better time?"
- Identify trigger events
- Set future follow-up
- Stay top-of-mind with nurture
```

**"We need approval from [person]"**:
```
Response: "No problem. Should we schedule time to present to them together?"
- Identify decision-making process
- Get introduced to decision-maker
- Provide materials for internal selling
```

### 7. Closing Techniques

**Trial Close**:
```
"If we can solve [pain point], would you be ready to move forward?"
"On a scale of 1-10, how confident are you this will solve your problem?"
```

**Assumptive Close**:
```
"When would you like to start implementation?"
"Should we schedule the kickoff for next Monday or Wednesday?"
```

**Summary Close**:
```
"So we've agreed on [feature 1], [feature 2], and [pricing]. Does that sound right?"
"Let me recap what we've covered..."
```

**Urgency Close**:
```
"Our Q4 implementation slots are filling up. To guarantee a November start, we'd need to finalize by Friday."
"Current pricing expires at month-end."
```

**Takeaway Close**:
```
"I'm not sure this is the right fit for you right now..."
(Often triggers buyer to re-engage)
```

### 8. Account Management & Retention

**Customer Success Framework**:

**Onboarding** (0-90 days):
- Kickoff call
- Technical setup
- User training
- Success metrics definition

**Adoption** (90-180 days):
- Usage monitoring
- Feature adoption
- Quick wins delivery
- Regular check-ins (monthly)

**Expansion** (180+ days):
- Quarterly business reviews (QBRs)
- Upsell/cross-sell opportunities
- Renewal preparation
- Reference program (case studies)

**Health Score Calculation**:
```
Health Score (0-100):

Product Usage (40 points):
- Login frequency: 15 pts
- Feature adoption: 15 pts
- API usage: 10 pts

Engagement (30 points):
- Support tickets: 10 pts (fewer = better)
- Training completion: 10 pts
- Check-in attendance: 10 pts

Business Value (30 points):
- ROI delivered: 15 pts
- NPS score: 10 pts
- Expansion potential: 5 pts

Health Tiers:
80-100: Green (healthy, upsell ready)
60-79: Yellow (needs attention)
< 60: Red (churn risk)
```

**Churn Prevention**:
- Early warning signs (usage drop, support tickets)
- Proactive outreach
- Executive sponsorship
- Win-back offers

- Calls/emails per day: 30-50
- Meetings booked per week: 5-10
- Demos per week: 3-5

**Pipeline Metrics**:
- Pipeline coverage: 3-4× quota
- Average deal size: $50K-$500K (manufacturing B2B)
- Sales velocity: Pipeline value × Win rate / Sales cycle length

**Performance Metrics**:
- Quota attainment: ≥ 100%

- Average sales cycle: 90-180 days

**Revenue Metrics**:
- Annual Contract Value (ACV)
- Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)

## Best Practices

1. **Always Be Helping** (not selling)
   - Solve problems
   - Provide value
   - Build trust

2. **Listen More Than You Talk**
   - 70% listening, 30% talking
   - Ask open-ended questions
   - Take notes

3. **Qualify Hard, Close Easy**
   - Thorough discovery
   - Confirm budget, authority, need, timeline
   - Disqualify bad fits early

4. **Follow Up Relentlessly**
   - 80% of sales require 5+ touchpoints
   - Schedule next steps before leaving meeting
   - Multi-channel outreach (email, phone, LinkedIn)
